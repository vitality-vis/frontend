The targeted workflow of literature research

## Procedure

Standalone review:

1. Formulating the problem
2. Searching the literature
3. Screening for inclusion
4. Extracting data (reading and schematising)
5. Analysing and Synthesising

The process can be iterative.

Reference:

- A Framework for Guiding and Evaluating Literature Reviews (CAIS 2015)
    - Note: the original step 4 (assessing quality) is excluded here as it only applied for aggregative reviews

The procedure also works for general literature research beyond standalone review papers, but the process is often less rigorous.

## Key elements

- Goal/purpose
- Research questions
- Concept definition
- Scope/coverage
- Inclusion criteria
- Papers
- Categories/Codebook
- Encodings
- Annotations and notes

## Workflow

- Onboarding
    - Rough understanding of key concepts,
    - size and scope of existing literature,
    - a few papers: existing survey, fundamental paper, recent work
    - The result is an initial understanding, which can be incomplete or inaccurate, but serves as a start point for the next steps.
- Exploration
    - Identify questions from the onboarding result
    - Expand the search: extend keywords/queries/filters, expand from seed papers, explore a specific collection (e.g., last year's X conference)
    - Informal screening: save papers that might be relevant
    - Selective reading: develop personal understanding, collect evidence (for concept/scope definition, etc.), etc.
    - Chatting: ask questions, find evidence to hypotheses, etc.
    - Start to form ideas about scope definition and inclusion criteria

-> General literature research
- Evaluation
    - Evaluate the collected papers: coverage, representativeness, quality, etc.
    - Validate if any important works are missing
- Synthesizing
    - Form a narrative
    - Read or chat
    - The level of comprehension and schematisation depends on the task demands

-> Systematic literature review
- Formulation (explicitly)
    - Formulate goal, define concepts, scopes, and inclusion criteria
    - Formulate search strategy
- Collection
    - Execute search
    - Execute screening
- Coding
    - Define codebook (an exploratory and iterative process; involves reuse of the mental model developed in earlier stages)
    - Read and encode papers
- Synthesizing and writing
    - Synthesize findings
    - Write literature review

Note:
- Formulation:
    - Though formulation may not be included in less formal scenarios as an explicit stage, researchers usually form a rough mental models during the literature research process.
    - It would be useful to make this explicit even for a less formal workflow, which helps (1) orient human users, (2) steer AI agents, and (3) evaluate and validate results.
